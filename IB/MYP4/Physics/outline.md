## Unit 2 : motion 

### Distance and Displacement   
Distance: Total path traveled, scalar quantity   
Displacement: Straight-line distance from starting to ending point, vector quantity   
Distinguishes between overall travel and net movement   

### Speed and Velocity   
Speed: Rate of distance traveled per unit time, scalar quantity  
Velocity: Speed with a direction, vector quantity  
Importance in differentiating between uniform and changing motion  

### Acceleration   
Rate of change of velocity per unit time, vector quantity   
Can be positive (speeding up) or negative (slowing down, also called deceleration)    
Key factor in understanding changes in motion   

### Motion Graphs 
Visual representations of motion over time   
Common types: Distance-time, velocity-time, and acceleration-time graphs   
Provide insight into an object's speed, direction, and acceleration   

### Interpretation of Motion Graphs   
Distance-time graph: Slope represents speed; a steeper slope indicates higher speed   
Velocity-time graph: Slope represents acceleration; area under the curve represents displacement   
Acceleration-time graph: Shows changes in acceleration over time, with are with area under the curve indicating change in velocity
