
# S5 Maths — Topics Revision Guide

## Overview

This guide covers all 14 topics of the S5 Maths curriculum, organised into **6 thematic parts**.

| Part | Theme | Topics |
|------|-------|--------|
| **I** | Arithmetic & Number | Percentages, Exponential Growth, Standard Form, Significant Figures, Laws of Indices |
| **II** | Algebra | Algebraic Manipulation, Quadratic Equations |
| **III** | Geometry & Vectors | Vectors (2D & 3D) |
| **IV** | Trigonometry | Trig Ratios, Trig Functions, Degrees & Radians |
| **V** | Statistics | Measures of Central Tendency & Spread |
| **VI** | Modelling & Reasoning | Graphs & Mathematical Modelling, Mathematical Reasoning |

**Calculator policy** is indicated for each topic. Where a topic appears in both calculator and no-calculator tests, the specific focus for each is noted.

---

---

# PART I — Arithmetic & Number

---

## 1. Percentages & Reverse Percentages

**Skills required:**
- Understanding percentage increase and decrease
- Reverse percentage calculations (finding the original value)
- Translating word problems into calculations

> **Test format:** No-calculator — focus on conceptual understanding and algebraic manipulation

---

## 2. Exponential Growth & Compound Interest

**Skills required:**
- Interpreting exponential models
- Substituting values into formulas
- Using tables to track exponential growth
- Interpreting results in context (e.g. "at least €6000")

> **Test format:** Calculator and no-calculator

---

## 3. Standard Form (Scientific Notation)

**Skills required:**
- Writing numbers in standard form
- Multiplying and dividing numbers in standard form
- Using powers of 10 correctly
- Rounding to significant figures
- Interpreting scale and magnitude

> **Test format:** Calculator and no-calculator — no-calculator focuses on conceptual understanding

---

## 4. Significant Figures & Accuracy

**Skills required:**
- Rounding numerical answers to a given number of significant figures
- Understanding accuracy requirements
- Presenting final answers correctly

> **Test format:** No-calculator

---

## 5. Laws of Indices (Exponents)

**Skills required:**
- Multiplying powers
- Dividing powers
- Power of a power
- Negative indices
- Fractional indices
- Simplifying algebraic expressions involving indices

> **Test format:** Calculator only — requires algebraic manipulation skills

---

---

# PART II — Algebra

---

## 6. Algebraic Manipulation & Solving Equations

**Skills required:**
- Expanding brackets
- Rearranging equations
- Solving linear equations
- Isolating a variable
- Solving equations involving radicals

> **Test format:** Calculator only

---

## 7. Quadratic Equations

**Skills required:**
- Recognising quadratic equations
- Solving by factorisation
- Interpreting solutions in context

> **Test format:** Calculator only

---

---

# PART III — Geometry & Vectors

---

## 8. Vectors (2D & 3D)

**Skills required:**
- Vector notation
- Column vectors
- Linear combinations
- Vector addition and subtraction
- Position vectors
- Solving vector equations
- Linear independence
- Basis of $\mathbb{R}^n$

> **Test format:** Calculator and no-calculator — no-calculator focuses on conceptual and algebraic reasoning; calculator includes graphical work

---

---

# PART IV — Trigonometry

---

## 9. Trigonometric Ratios

**Skills required:**
- SOH-CAH-TOA: finding missing sides and angles in right-angled triangles
- Knowing exact values of sin, cos, tan for special angles
- Special angles: $0°,\ 30°,\ 45°,\ 60°,\ 90°$
- Understanding undefined values (e.g. $\tan 90°$)
- Applying trig ratios in context (geometry problems, parallelograms, ramps)

> **Test format:** Calculator only

---

## 10. Trigonometric Functions (Graphs & Properties)

**Skills required:**
- Amplitude
- Period
- Vertical shift (principal axis)
- Phase shift
- Evaluating trig functions
- Graphing sine and cosine
- Interpreting trig models in context

> **Test format:** Calculator and no-calculator — no-calculator focuses on properties; calculator includes modelling

---

## 11. Angles: Degrees, Radians & Unit Circle

**Skills required:**
- Converting degrees to radians (and back)
- Understanding full rotations
- Drawing angles on the unit circle
- Recognising quadrants and direction of rotation

> **Test format:** Calculator only

---

---

# PART V — Statistics

---

## 12. Statistics: Measures of Central Tendency & Spread

**Skills required:**
- Calculating the mean from raw data and frequency tables
- Finding the mode and modal class
- Finding the median and understanding its position
- Calculating the range
- Calculating the interquartile range (IQR)
- Calculating standard deviation
- Interpreting standard deviation in context
- Comparing data sets using measures of centre and spread

> **Test format:** Calculator and no-calculator — emphasis on interpretation and comparison, not just computation

---

---

# PART VI — Modelling & Mathematical Reasoning

---

## 13. Graphs & Mathematical Modelling

**Skills required:**
- Completing tables of values
- Plotting graphs accurately
- Identifying function types (linear, quadratic, exponential, …)
- Interpreting graphs
- Estimating values from graphs
- Evaluating the limitations of a mathematical model

> **Test format:** Calculator only

---

## 14. Mathematical Reasoning & Justification

**Skills required:**
- Explaining and justifying answers clearly
- Justifying conclusions verbally
- Interpreting formulas conceptually
- Understanding the effect of changing parameters in a model

> **Test format:** Calculator and no-calculator — focuses on conceptual understanding and mathematical communication
