### A. Right Triangle Trigonometry

**Exercise 1** — angle = 20°, hypotenuse = 15 cm

a) Opposite side:
$$\sin(20°) = \frac{\text{opp}}{15}$$
$$\text{opp} = 15 \times \sin(20°) = 15 \times 0.342 \approx \boxed{5.13 \text{ cm}}$$

b) Adjacent side:
$$\cos(20°) = \frac{\text{adj}}{15}$$
$$\text{adj} = 15 \times \cos(20°) = 15 \times 0.940 \approx \boxed{14.10 \text{ cm}}$$

---

**Exercise 2** — distance = 50 m, angle of elevation = 62°

$$\tan(62°) = \frac{\text{height}}{50}$$
$$\text{height} = 50 \times \tan(62°) = 50 \times 1.8807 \approx \boxed{94.0 \text{ m}}$$

---

**Exercise 3** — opposite = 9, adjacent = 12

a) Find $\theta$:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{9}{12} = 0.75$$
$$\theta = \arctan(0.75) \approx \boxed{36.9°}$$

b) The other acute angle:
Since the three angles of a triangle sum to $180°$, and one is $90°$:
$$\text{other angle} = 90° - 36.9° = \boxed{53.1°}$$

---

**Exercise 4** — 30 km east, 40 km north

a) Straight-line distance (Pythagoras):
$$d = \sqrt{30^2 + 40^2} = \sqrt{900 + 1600} = \sqrt{2500} = \boxed{50 \text{ km}}$$

b) Angle from north (looking south-west):
The horizontal component is 30 km (west), the vertical is 40 km (south).
$$\tan(\alpha) = \frac{30}{40} = 0.75 \implies \alpha = \arctan(0.75) \approx \boxed{36.9° \text{ west of south}}$$

**Exercise 2** — Equilateral triangle, side = 2, cut in half

Cutting the equilateral triangle in half gives a right triangle with:
- Hypotenuse = 2 (original side)
- Base = 1 (half the bottom side)
- Height = $\sqrt{2^2 - 1^2} = \sqrt{3}$

The 60° angle is at the top:
$$\sin(60°) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}, \quad \cos(60°) = \frac{1}{2}, \quad \tan(60°) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{1} = \sqrt{3}$$

The 30° angle is at the bottom:
$$\sin(30°) = \frac{1}{2}, \quad \cos(30°) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}, \quad \tan(30°) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$$

---

**Exercise 3** — Evaluate without a calculator

- $\dfrac{\sin(30°)}{\cos(30°)} = \dfrac{1/2}{\sqrt{3}/2} = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \boxed{\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{3}}$
  *(This equals $\tan(30°)$ — a reminder that $\tan = \sin/\cos$)*

- $\cos^2(45°) = \left(\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)^2 = \dfrac{2}{4} = \boxed{\dfrac{1}{2}}$

- $\sin(90°) - \cos(0°) = 1 - 1 = \boxed{0}$

**Exercise 4** — Is $\tan(90°)$ equal to 1 or undefined?

**Student B is correct** — $\tan(90°)$ is **undefined**.

Explanation:
$$\tan(90°) = \frac{\sin(90°)}{\cos(90°)} = \frac{1}{0}$$
Division by zero is undefined.

On the unit circle: at $90°$, the point is $(0, 1)$. The tangent is the $y/x$ ratio $= 1/0$, which has no value. Alternatively, the tangent line to the circle at this point is horizontal and never meets the $x$-axis — so there is no finite answer.

### D. Trigonometric Functions & Graphs

**Exercise 1** — $y = \dfrac{1}{2}\sin(4x) - 2$

- **Amplitude** = $\boxed{\dfrac{1}{2}}$
- **Period** = $\dfrac{360°}{4} = \boxed{90°}$
- **Vertical shift** = $\boxed{-2}$ (down 2 units)

---

**Exercise 2** — $y = \sin(x)$ and $y = \sin(x) + 2$

Key points of $y = \sin(x) + 2$:

| $x$ | $0°$ | $90°$ | $180°$ | $270°$ | $360°$ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $y$ | $1$ | $3$ | $1$ | $-1$ | $1$ |

**Transformation**: $y = \sin(x) + 2$ is the graph of $y = \sin(x)$ shifted vertically **up by 2 units**. The shape, amplitude, and period are identical.

---

**Exercise 3** — $V = 240\sin(100\pi t)$

a) Peak voltage:
$$V_{\max} = \boxed{240 \text{ V}}$$

b) Period:
$$T = \frac{2\pi}{100\pi} = \frac{2}{100} = \frac{1}{50} \approx \boxed{0.02 \text{ s}}$$

c) Frequency:
$$f = \frac{1}{T} = \boxed{50 \text{ Hz (cycles per second)}}$$
This is standard mains AC frequency used in Europe and Australia.

---

3. $y = \sin(x) + 5$ → **A** (Vertical shift up 5)
