## Trigonometry - practice sheet - 02

### A. Right Triangle Trigonometry
**Exercise 1 — Basic triangle**
A right triangle has:
• angle = $50^\circ$
• hypotenuse = 10 cm
Draw the triangle and find:
a) the opposite side
b) the adjacent side

**Exercise 2 — Tree problem**
A tree casts a shadow of 12 m on flat ground.
The angle of elevation of the sun is $40^\circ$.
Draw the situation and find the height of the tree.

**Exercise 3 — Angle finding**
In a right triangle:
• opposite = 5
• hypotenuse = 13
Draw the triangle and find $\theta$.

**Exercise 4 — Mixed**
A kite string of length 25 m makes an angle of $55^\circ$ with the ground.
Draw the situation and find:
a) the height of the kite above the ground
b) the horizontal distance from the person to a point directly below the kite

a. $\sin(\theta) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$

**Exercise 2**

### D. Trigonometric Functions & Graphs
**Exercise 1 — Identify parameters**
Given: $y = 4 \cos(3x) + 1$
Find:
• amplitude
• period
• vertical shift

**Exercise 2 — Graphing**
Sketch the graph of:
$y = \sin(2x)$ for $0 \le x \le 360^\circ$
Label the key points clearly.

**Exercise 3 — Real-life model**
The temperature (in °C) in a city is modelled by:
$T = 12 \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}(m - 3)\right) + 15$
where $m$ is the month number (1 = January).
Find:
a) the maximum and minimum temperature
b) the period of the model
c) in which month is the temperature at its maximum?

**Exercise 4 — Interpretation**
A sinusoidal graph has:
• amplitude = 3
• midline = $-1$
• period = $180^\circ$
Write a possible equation using $\sin$.

### E. Angles, Radians & Unit Circle
**Exercise 1 — Conversions**
Convert to radians:
• $60^\circ$
• $120^\circ$
• $270^\circ$

Convert to degrees:
• $\frac{\pi}{4}$
• $\frac{3\pi}{2}$
• $\frac{2\pi}{3}$

**Exercise 2 — Unit circle basics**
State the coordinates of the point on the unit circle for:
• $30^\circ$
• $60^\circ$
• $135^\circ$
• $210^\circ$

**Exercise 3 — Exact values using unit circle**
Find:
• $\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\right)$
• $\cos\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$
• $\tan\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}\right)$

**Exercise 4 — Mixed**
An angle is $315^\circ$.
Find:
• its radian measure
• $\sin(\theta)$ and $\cos(\theta)$ using the unit circle
• In which quadrant does this angle lie?
