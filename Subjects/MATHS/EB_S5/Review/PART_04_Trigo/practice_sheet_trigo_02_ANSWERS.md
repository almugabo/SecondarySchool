### A. Right Triangle Trigonometry

**Exercise 1** — angle = 50°, hypotenuse = 10 cm

a) Opposite side:
$$\sin(50°) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{10}$$
$$\text{opp} = 10 \times \sin(50°) = 10 \times 0.766 \approx \boxed{7.66 \text{ cm}}$$

b) Adjacent side:
$$\cos(50°) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{10}$$
$$\text{adj} = 10 \times \cos(50°) = 10 \times 0.643 \approx \boxed{6.43 \text{ cm}}$$

---

**Exercise 2** — shadow = 12 m, angle of elevation = 40°

$$\tan(40°) = \frac{\text{height}}{12}$$
$$\text{height} = 12 \times \tan(40°) = 12 \times 0.839 \approx \boxed{10.07 \text{ m}}$$

---

**Exercise 3** — opposite = 5, hypotenuse = 13

$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{5}{13} \approx 0.3846$$
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.3846) \approx \boxed{22.6°}$$

---

**Exercise 4** — kite string = 25 m, angle = 55°

a) Height of kite:
$$\sin(55°) = \frac{h}{25}$$
$$h = 25 \times \sin(55°) = 25 \times 0.819 \approx \boxed{20.5 \text{ m}}$$

b) Horizontal distance:
$$\cos(55°) = \frac{d}{25}$$
$$d = 25 \times \cos(55°) = 25 \times 0.574 \approx \boxed{14.3 \text{ m}}$$

### C. Trigonometric Equations & Angles

**Exercise 1** — solve in $[0°, 360°]$

a) $\sin(\theta) = \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
Reference angle = $\arcsin\!\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right) = 45°$
sin positive in Q1 and Q2:
$$\boxed{\theta = 45° \text{ or } 135°}$$

b) $\cos(\theta) = 0$
cos = 0 at the top and bottom of the unit circle:
$$\boxed{\theta = 90° \text{ or } 270°}$$

c) $\tan(\theta) = \sqrt{3}$
Reference angle = $\arctan(\sqrt{3}) = 60°$
tan positive in Q1 and Q3:
$$\boxed{\theta = 60° \text{ or } 240°}$$

### D. Trigonometric Functions & Graphs

**Exercise 1** — $y = 4\cos(3x) + 1$

- **Amplitude** = $\boxed{4}$
- **Period** = $\dfrac{360°}{3} = \boxed{120°}$
- **Vertical shift** = $\boxed{+1}$ (up 1 unit)

---

**Exercise 2** — $y = \sin(2x)$ for $0° \le x \le 360°$

- Amplitude = 1, Period = $180°$
- Key points every $45°$:

| $x$ | $0°$ | $45°$ | $90°$ | $135°$ | $180°$ | $225°$ | $270°$ | $315°$ | $360°$ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $y$ | $0$ | $1$ | $0$ | $-1$ | $0$ | $1$ | $0$ | $-1$ | $0$ |

Two full cycles fit between $0°$ and $360°$.

---

**Exercise 3** — $T = 12\sin\!\left(\dfrac{\pi}{6}(m-3)\right) + 15$

a) Maximum and minimum temperature:
$$T_{\max} = 15 + 12 = \boxed{27°\text{C}}$$
$$T_{\min} = 15 - 12 = \boxed{3°\text{C}}$$

b) Period:
$$T = \frac{2\pi}{\pi/6} = \boxed{12 \text{ months}}$$
This makes sense — a full year cycle.

c) Maximum when $\sin = 1$:
$$\frac{\pi}{6}(m - 3) = \frac{\pi}{2} \implies m - 3 = 3 \implies m = 6$$
$$\boxed{\text{Maximum temperature in June (month 6)}}$$

---

**Exercise 4** — amplitude = 3, midline = −1, period = 180°

Period = $\dfrac{360°}{b} = 180°$ so $b = 2$.
$$\boxed{y = 3\sin(2x) - 1}$$
(Any sine or cosine with these parameters is valid.)

### E. Angles, Radians & Unit Circle

**Exercise 1** — Conversions

To radians:
- $60° = \boxed{\dfrac{\pi}{3}}$
- $120° = \boxed{\dfrac{2\pi}{3}}$
- $270° = \boxed{\dfrac{3\pi}{2}}$

To degrees:
- $\dfrac{\pi}{4} = \boxed{45°}$
- $\dfrac{3\pi}{2} = \boxed{270°}$
- $\dfrac{2\pi}{3} = \boxed{120°}$

---

**Exercise 2** — Unit circle coordinates

- $30°$: $\boxed{\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2},\ \dfrac{1}{2}\right)}$
- $60°$: $\boxed{\left(\dfrac{1}{2},\ \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)}$
- $135°$: $\boxed{\left(-\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2},\ \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)}$
- $210°$: $\boxed{\left(-\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2},\ -\dfrac{1}{2}\right)}$

---

**Exercise 3** — Exact values

- $\sin\!\left(\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) = \sin(30°) = \boxed{\dfrac{1}{2}}$
- $\cos\!\left(\dfrac{2\pi}{3}\right) = \cos(120°) = -\cos(60°) = \boxed{-\dfrac{1}{2}}$
- $\tan\!\left(\dfrac{3\pi}{4}\right) = \tan(135°) = -\tan(45°) = \boxed{-1}$

---

**Exercise 4** — $315°$

- Radian measure: $315 \times \dfrac{\pi}{180} = \boxed{\dfrac{7\pi}{4}}$
- $315°$ is in Q4; reference angle = $360° - 315° = 45°$
$$\sin(315°) = -\sin(45°) = \boxed{-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}}$$
$$\cos(315°) = +\cos(45°) = \boxed{+\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}}$$
- Coordinates on unit circle: $\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2},\ -\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)$
