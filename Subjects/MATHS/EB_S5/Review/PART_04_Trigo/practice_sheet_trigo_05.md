## Trigonometry - practice sheet - 05

### A. Right Triangle Trigonometry
**Exercise 1 — Basic triangle**
A right triangle has:
• angle = $43^\circ$
• adjacent side = 11 cm
Draw the triangle and find:
a) the opposite side
b) the hypotenuse

**Exercise 2 — Flagpole problem**
A flagpole stands vertically.
From a point 18 m away on the ground, the angle of elevation to the top is $52^\circ$.
Draw the situation and find the height of the flagpole.

**Exercise 3 — Angle finding**
In a right triangle:
• opposite = 11
• hypotenuse = 20
Draw the triangle and find $\theta$.
Then find the size of the third angle without a calculator.

**Exercise 4 — Mixed**
A wheelchair ramp must rise 0.9 m to a doorway.
Safety regulations require the ramp to make an angle no greater than $12^\circ$ with the ground.
Draw the situation and find:
a) the minimum horizontal length of the ramp
b) the minimum length of the sloping surface

• hypotenuse = $2\sqrt{2}$
• one leg = 2
Find the other leg and then calculate $\sin(\theta)$, $\cos(\theta)$, $\tan(\theta)$ for both acute angles.

**Exercise 3 — Exact value**
Without a calculator, simplify:
• $\sin(30^\circ) \cdot \cos(60^\circ) + \cos(30^\circ) \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$

### C. Trigonometric Equations & Angles
**Exercise 1**
Solve for $\theta$ in $[0^\circ, 360^\circ]$:
a. $\sin(\theta) = -1$
b. $\cos(\theta) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
c. $\tan(\theta) = 0$

**Exercise 2**
Solve:
a. $3\sin(\theta) = -3$
b. $4\cos(\theta) = 2\sqrt{2}$
c. $\tan(\theta) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$

**Exercise 3 — Mixed**
a. Solve $\sin(x) = 0.42$ for $0^\circ \le x \le 360^\circ$.
b. Solve $\cos(x) = -0.9$ for $0^\circ \le x \le 360^\circ$.

### D. Trigonometric Functions & Graphs
**Exercise 1 — Identify parameters**
Given: $y = 6 \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{3} x\right) - 1$
Find:
• amplitude
• period
• vertical shift

**Exercise 2 — Graphing**
Sketch the graph of:
$y = 3\sin(x) + 1$ for $0 \le x \le 360^\circ$
Clearly mark the maximum, minimum, and the midline.

**Exercise 3 — Real-life model**
A buoy bobs up and down in the sea.
Its height above the seabed (in metres) is modelled by:
$h = 3 \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) + 8$
where $t$ is in seconds.
Find:
a) the maximum and minimum heights
b) the period of the motion
c) at what times in the first 16 seconds is $h = 8$?

**Exercise 4 — Interpretation**
Two students graph different functions:
• Student A: $y = 2\sin(x)$
• Student B: $y = \sin(2x)$
a) What is different about their graphs? (amplitude? period? midline?)
b) At $x = 90^\circ$, which function has the greater value? Justify.

• $\frac{\pi}{3}$
• $\frac{3\pi}{4}$

**Exercise 3 — Exact values using unit circle**
Find:
• $\sin\left(\frac{7\pi}{6}\right)$
• $\cos\left(\frac{5\pi}{3}\right)$
• $\tan\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}\right)$

**Exercise 4 — Mixed**
a) Convert $-120^\circ$ to its equivalent positive angle in $[0^\circ, 360^\circ]$.

c) Find the exact values of $\sin$, $\cos$, and $\tan$ for this angle.
