## Trigonometry - practice sheet - 03

### A. Right Triangle Trigonometry
**Exercise 1 — Basic triangle**
A right triangle has:
• angle = $72^\circ$
• opposite side = 9 cm
Draw the triangle and find:
a) the adjacent side
b) the hypotenuse

**Exercise 2 — Roof problem**
A roof has a horizontal span of 8 m.
The roof rises at an angle of $25^\circ$ from the horizontal.
Draw the situation and find the length of the sloping roof.

**Exercise 3 — Angle finding**
In a right triangle:
• adjacent = 6
• hypotenuse = 10
Draw the triangle and find $\theta$.

**Exercise 4 — Mixed**
Two buildings stand opposite each other.
From the top of a 20 m building, the angle of depression to the base of the other building is $38^\circ$.
Draw the situation and find the horizontal distance between the two buildings.

### C. Trigonometric Equations & Angles
**Exercise 1**
Solve for $\theta$ in $[0^\circ, 360^\circ]$:
a. $\sin(\theta) = -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
b. $\cos(\theta) = \frac{1}{2}$
c. $\tan(\theta) = -\sqrt{3}$

**Exercise 2**
Solve:
a. $2\sin(\theta) = 1$
b. $2\cos(\theta) + \sqrt{3} = 0$
c. $\tan(\theta) + 1 = 0$

**Exercise 3 — Mixed**
Solve for $x$ in $[0^\circ, 360^\circ]$:
$\sin(x) = -0.342$

State both solutions clearly and explain which quadrants they lie in.

### D. Trigonometric Functions & Graphs
**Exercise 1 — Identify parameters**
Given: $y = -2 \sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + 4$
Find:
• amplitude
• period
• vertical shift
• Does the graph flip compared to a standard sine? Explain.

**Exercise 2 — Graphing**
Sketch the graph of:
$y = -\cos(x)$ for $0 \le x \le 360^\circ$
Describe in one sentence how it differs from $y = \cos(x)$.

**Exercise 3 — Real-life model**
A Ferris wheel has a diameter of 20 m.
Its centre is 12 m above the ground.
It completes one revolution every 40 seconds.
A rider starts at the bottom.
Write an equation for the rider's height $h$ (in metres) as a function of time $t$ (in seconds).

**Exercise 4 — Interpretation**
The graph of a function reaches a maximum of 7 and a minimum of 1.
a) Find the amplitude and midline.
b) Write a possible $\sin$ equation if the period is $360^\circ$.

**Exercise 4 — Mixed**
