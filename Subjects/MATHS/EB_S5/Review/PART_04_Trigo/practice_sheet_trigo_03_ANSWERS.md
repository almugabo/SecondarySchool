### A. Right Triangle Trigonometry

**Exercise 1** — angle = 72°, opposite = 9 cm

a) Adjacent side:
$$\tan(72°) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{9}{\text{adj}}$$
$$\text{adj} = \frac{9}{\tan(72°)} = \frac{9}{3.0777} \approx \boxed{2.92 \text{ cm}}$$

b) Hypotenuse:
$$\sin(72°) = \frac{9}{\text{hyp}}$$
$$\text{hyp} = \frac{9}{\sin(72°)} = \frac{9}{0.9511} \approx \boxed{9.46 \text{ cm}}$$

---

**Exercise 2** — horizontal span = 8 m, angle = 25°

The horizontal distance is the adjacent side, the sloping roof is the hypotenuse.
$$\cos(25°) = \frac{8}{\text{hyp}}$$
$$\text{hyp} = \frac{8}{\cos(25°)} = \frac{8}{0.9063} \approx \boxed{8.83 \text{ m}}$$

---

**Exercise 3** — adjacent = 6, hypotenuse = 10

$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{6}{10} = 0.6$$
$$\theta = \arccos(0.6) \approx \boxed{53.1°}$$

---

**Exercise 4** — height = 20 m, angle of depression = 38°

The angle of depression from the top equals the angle of elevation from the base = 38°.
The height is the opposite side, the horizontal distance is the adjacent side.
$$\tan(38°) = \frac{20}{d}$$
$$d = \frac{20}{\tan(38°)} = \frac{20}{0.7813} \approx \boxed{25.6 \text{ m}}$$

**Exercise 2** — Isosceles right triangle, legs = 1

By Pythagoras: hypotenuse $= \sqrt{1^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{2}$

$$\sin(45°) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = \boxed{\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}}$$
$$\cos(45°) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = \boxed{\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}}$$
$$\tan(45°) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{1}{1} = \boxed{1}$$

---

**Exercise 3** — Evaluate without a calculator

- $\sin^2(30°) + \cos^2(30°) = \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^2 + \left(\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^2 = \dfrac{1}{4} + \dfrac{3}{4} = \boxed{1}$
  *(This is always 1 — the Pythagorean identity)*

- $\sin(60°) \times \cos(30°) = \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \dfrac{3}{4} = \boxed{0.75}$

- $\tan(60°) \times \tan(30°) = \sqrt{3} \times \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \boxed{1}$

### D. Trigonometric Functions & Graphs

**Exercise 1** — $y = -2\sin\!\left(\dfrac{x}{2}\right) + 4$

- **Amplitude** = $\boxed{2}$ (always the absolute value of the coefficient)
- **Period** = $\dfrac{360°}{1/2} = \boxed{720°}$
- **Vertical shift** = $\boxed{+4}$ (up 4 units)
- **Reflection**: Yes — the negative sign means the graph is flipped vertically. It starts at its *minimum* rather than its maximum (compared to a standard $\sin$).

---

**Exercise 2** — $y = -\cos(x)$

Key points:

| $x$ | $0°$ | $90°$ | $180°$ | $270°$ | $360°$ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $y$ | $-1$ | $0$ | $1$ | $0$ | $-1$ |

One-sentence description: **The graph of $y = -\cos(x)$ is a reflection of $y = \cos(x)$ in the $x$-axis — it starts at a minimum and reaches its maximum at $180°$.**

---

**Exercise 3** — Ferris wheel: diameter = 20 m, centre = 12 m, period = 40 s, starts at bottom

- Amplitude = $\dfrac{20}{2} = 10$ m (radius)
- Midline = $12$ m (height of centre)
- Period = $40$ s $\implies b = \dfrac{2\pi}{40} = \dfrac{\pi}{20}$
- Starting at the bottom means the function starts at its minimum → use $-\cos$

$$\boxed{h = -10\cos\!\left(\frac{\pi}{20}\,t\right) + 12}$$

Check: at $t = 0$: $h = -10(1) + 12 = 2$ m ✓ (bottom of wheel = $12 - 10 = 2$ m)
