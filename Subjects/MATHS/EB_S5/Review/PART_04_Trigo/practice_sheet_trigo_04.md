## Trigonometry - practice sheet - 04

### A. Right Triangle Trigonometry
**Exercise 1 — Basic triangle**
A right triangle has:
• angle = $20^\circ$
• hypotenuse = 15 cm
Draw the triangle and find:
a) the opposite side
b) the adjacent side

**Exercise 2 — Surveying problem**
A surveyor stands 50 m from the base of a cliff.
The angle of elevation to the top of the cliff is $62^\circ$.
Draw the situation and find the height of the cliff.

**Exercise 3 — Two angles**
In a right triangle, one acute angle is $\theta$.
• opposite = 9
• adjacent = 12
a) Find $\theta$.
b) Without extra calculation, write down the value of the other acute angle.

**Exercise 4 — Mixed**
A ship sails 30 km due east, then turns and sails 40 km due north.
Draw the situation and find:
a) the straight-line distance from the start point to the finish point
b) the angle the return path makes with the north direction

### D. Trigonometric Functions & Graphs
**Exercise 1 — Identify parameters**
Given: $y = \frac{1}{2} \sin(4x) - 2$
Find:
• amplitude
• period
• vertical shift

**Exercise 2 — Graphing**
On the same axes, sketch:
• $y = \sin(x)$
• $y = \sin(x) + 2$
Describe in one sentence the transformation applied.

**Exercise 3 — Real-life model**
The voltage in an electrical circuit is modelled by:
$V = 240 \sin(100\pi t)$
where $t$ is in seconds.
Find:
a) the peak voltage
b) the period of the wave
c) the frequency (number of cycles per second)

**Exercise 4 — Interpretation**
Match each equation to its description:
1. $y = 5\sin(x)$  
2. $y = \sin(5x)$  
3. $y = \sin(x) + 5$  
4. $y = \sin(x - 5)$  

A. Vertical shift up 5  
B. Amplitude of 5  
C. Period is $\frac{2\pi}{5}$  
D. Horizontal shift right by 5
