### A. Right Triangle Trigonometry

**Exercise 1** — angle = 35°, adjacent = 8 cm

a) Opposite side:
$$\tan(35°) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{\text{opp}}{8}$$
$$\text{opp} = 8 \times \tan(35°) = 8 \times 0.7002 \approx \boxed{5.60 \text{ cm}}$$

b) Hypotenuse:
$$\cos(35°) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{8}{\text{hyp}}$$
$$\text{hyp} = \frac{8}{\cos(35°)} = \frac{8}{0.8192} \approx \boxed{9.76 \text{ cm}}$$

---

**Exercise 2** — ladder 6 m, angle 60° with the ground

The height reached = opposite side relative to 60°.
$$\sin(60°) = \frac{\text{height}}{6}$$
$$\text{height} = 6 \times \sin(60°) = 6 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = 3\sqrt{3} \approx \boxed{5.20 \text{ m}}$$

---

**Exercise 3** — opposite = 7, adjacent = 10

$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{7}{10} = 0.7$$
$$\theta = \arctan(0.7) \approx \boxed{35.0°}$$

---

**Exercise 4** — ramp: rises 0.5 m over 3 m horizontal

a) Length of the ramp (hypotenuse):
$$\text{hyp} = \sqrt{3^2 + 0.5^2} = \sqrt{9 + 0.25} = \sqrt{9.25} \approx \boxed{3.04 \text{ m}}$$

b) Angle of inclination:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{0.5}{3} = 0.1\overline{6}$$
$$\theta = \arctan(0.1667) \approx \boxed{9.5°}$$

**Exercise 3** — $d = 4\cos\!\left(\dfrac{\pi}{6}t + \pi\right) + 10$

a) Maximum and minimum depth:
$$d_{\max} = 10 + 4 = \boxed{14 \text{ m}}$$
$$d_{\min} = 10 - 4 = \boxed{6 \text{ m}}$$

b) Period:
$$T = \frac{2\pi}{\pi/6} = \boxed{12 \text{ hours}}$$

c) When $d \ge 9$:
$$4\cos\!\left(\frac{\pi}{6}t + \pi\right) + 10 \ge 9$$
$$\cos\!\left(\frac{\pi}{6}t + \pi\right) \ge -\frac{1}{4}$$
Let $u = \frac{\pi}{6}t + \pi$. Then $\cos(u) = -0.25$ at $u \approx 1.823$ rad and $u \approx 4.460$ rad.
Converting back: $t = \frac{6}{\pi}(u - \pi)$
$$t_1 = \frac{6}{\pi}(1.823 - \pi) \approx -2.52 \quad \Rightarrow \quad t \approx 9.48 \text{ (adding one period)}$$
$$t_2 = \frac{6}{\pi}(4.460 - \pi) \approx 2.52$$
$$\boxed{d \ge 9 \text{ m when } 0 \le t \le 2.52 \text{ h or } 9.48 \le t \le 12 \text{ h (per cycle)}}$$

### E. Angles, Radians & Unit Circle

**Exercise 1** — Conversions

To radians (multiply by $\frac{\pi}{180}$):
- $45° = \dfrac{45\pi}{180} = \boxed{\dfrac{\pi}{4}}$
- $150° = \dfrac{150\pi}{180} = \boxed{\dfrac{5\pi}{6}}$
- $300° = \dfrac{300\pi}{180} = \boxed{\dfrac{5\pi}{3}}$

To degrees (multiply by $\frac{180}{\pi}$):
- $\dfrac{\pi}{6} = \dfrac{180°}{6} = \boxed{30°}$
- $\dfrac{5\pi}{4} = \dfrac{5 \times 180°}{4} = \boxed{225°}$
- $3\pi = 3 \times 180° = \boxed{540°}$

---
